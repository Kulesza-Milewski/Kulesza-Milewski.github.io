Internet of things

The IoT brings the power of the internet, data processing, and analytics, to the real world of physical objects—our everyday lives. For consumers, this means interacting with a global information network without the mediation of a computer keyboard or screen. It turns out that many everyday objects can download instructions from the internet, with minimal human intervention. In business settings, the IoT can provide the same efficiency in physical production and distribution that the internet has long provided for knowledge-based work. Millions (if not billions) of embedded, internet-connected sensors around the world provide incredible amounts of data that companies can freely leverage.

How does the Internet of Things work?

The foundation of IoT is data-collecting devices, each connected to the internet and with its own IP address. They differ only in complexity, some are autonomous vehicles transporting products around warehouses, while others are small sensors monitoring temperature in buildings. They also include personal fitness trackers, reporting daily distances traveled.

What benefits can it bring to your company?

Thanks to IoT, companies have access to more data about their products and internal systems, and therefore have a greater ability to implement changes and improvements. Many manufacturers are taking advantage of the ability to add sensors to manufactured components to obtain data on their performance. This allows them to more effectively detect failures, replacing components before they cause damage.
